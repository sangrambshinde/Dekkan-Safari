With its massive walls and imposing bastions, it was built to protect the Maratha Empire from naval attacks by the Dutch, British, and Portuguese.

History

SINDHUDURG FORT Tourism at Sindhudurg Fort and Tarkarli has evolved over the years. Initially, it was more of an offbeat destination known to history enthusiasts and the descendants of the Maratha Empire. Over the years, the Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation (MTDC) has taken numerous steps to promote Tarkarli and the Sindhudurg region, highlighting not only the fortress but also the stunning beaches and coastal culture of the Konkan coast. In the late 20th century, wider recognition through efforts like the "Clean Beaches" campaign and the development of water sports facilities saw a rise in domestic tourism. Sindhudurg Fort became particularly important as a site of national heritage, which attracted tourists who were interested in the history, culture, and architecture of the Maratha Empire.

Structure

The fort's structure includes:

  • Walls: 30 ft high and 12 ft thick, in a zigzag pattern
  • Bastions: 52 bastions with embrasures for cannons, some with secret exits
  • Towers: Two watch towers half a mile apart
  • Water wells: Four water wells for the occupants
  • Houses: About 150 houses inside the fort
  • Main gate: The Dilli Darwaja is the main entrance, but its location is concealed
