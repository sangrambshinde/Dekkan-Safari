Murud Janjira

____________________________________________________________________________________

Janjira Fort, also known as Murud-Janjira Fort, is a famous sea fort in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. It's located on a small island in the Arabian Sea, off the coast of the coastal town of Murud, about 165 kilometers south of Mumbai..

History

Murud Janjira Fort is a perfect reason to visit Raigad district in Maharashtra. The fort, locally referred to as Janjira Fort, dates back to the late 17th century, but some sources state that its history could be traced back to the 1100s. Back then, the fort served as a stronghold for the Siddis, who were formidable naval and military commanders in the region. This island fortress was surrounded by the Arabian Sea from all sides. This meant only one thing – conquering this fort was no child’s play.Murud Janjira Fort’s most prominent feature is its robust fortification. When we take the fort’s structure and location into consideration, it sure looks like something that’s quite impenetrable. And it was just that. The fort has never been conquered by outside forces. It changed hands only after it was handed over to the Indian government post independence from British rule in 1947.The fort is built on an oval-shaped rock and is spread across an area of approximately 22 acres. As expected from a military fort, its bastions, cannons, and strategically designed entrance points, are pretty impressive. It must have looked formidable to enemies.There is only one entrance to the fort. It’s through a massive gateway that’s adorned with inscriptions in Arabic. Inside the fort, you will find what’s left of a medieval mosque, a palace, granaries, and residential quarters for the soldiers. The palace was the residence of the Siddi Nawab.Murud Janjira Fort is open to the public, and can be an exciting addition to your Maharashtra itinerary. For those interested in history, this fort is a must-visit. As mentioned above, Murud Janjira Fort is surrounded by water on all sides. There is just one entrance, and tourists can reach it by taking a boat from the coastal town of Murud.

Structure

The fort's structure includes:

  • Walls: 40-foot-tall walls made of limestone, glass, and jaggery
  • Bastions: 28 bastions, 19 of which are still intact
  • Towers: 19 tall towers, with over 90 feet between each tower
  • Ramparts: Ramparts with arches and guns facing the arches
  • Cannons: Many cannons of native and European make rusting on the bastions
  • Main gate: Faces Rajapuri on the shore and can only be seen when one is close to it
  • Posterngate: A small posterngate towards the open sea for escape
