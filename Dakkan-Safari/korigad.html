KORIGAD FORT

Korigad Fort, located near Lonavala in the Indian state of Maharashtra, is a historic hill fort that dates back to the 17th century. Built by the Maratha warrior king Shivaji Maharaj, it served as a strategic stronghold to guard the western passes of the Sahyadri mountains. Perched atop a hill at an elevation of around 923 meters, Korigad offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Korigad fort, or “Koraigad,” is one of the most suitable treks in Maharashtra for someone who plans to start trekking. An effortless trek, offering the best views of the surrounding region. The trek is famous for mini steps-like waterfalls during monsoons, seasonal reverse waterfall & the vast plateau on top of the fort where you can even camp during the winter. You can cover the entire 2km perimeter of the fort on foot to enjoy the views of the whole region.

History

This fort was incorporated into his kingdom by the Maratha ruler Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj along with the forts of Lohagad, Visapur, Tung and Tikona in 1657. On 11 March 1818, Colonel Prother tried to take over this fort but even after a prolonged siege could not make any headway. Finally on 14 March, by igniting the stored ammunition by means of a loose cannonball he succeeded and this fort went to the British.

Korigad Fort, also known as Koraigad or Kumwarigad, is a hill fort located in the Western Ghats near Lonavala in Maharashtra, India. It has a rich history dating back to ancient times. Originally, the fort was built by the Koli dynasty, who were the original inhabitants of the region.In the 15th century, the fort came under the control of the Bahmani Sultanate. Later, it was captured by the Adil Shahi dynasty of Bijapur. During this time, the fort served as a strategic outpost to control the surrounding region.In the 17th century, the fort was captured and fortified by the Maratha ruler Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Under his rule, Korigad played a significant role in the Maratha Empire's defense system, serving as a watchtower to monitor the movements of enemy forces in the region.After Shivaji Maharaj's death, the fort exchanged hands several times between the Marathas, Mughals, and the British East India Company during various conflicts in the region.Today, Korigad Fort stands as a popular trekking destination, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and serving as a reminder of Maharashtra's rich historical heritage.



Structures on the Fort

A temple to its patron goddess Koraidevi also exists along with several smaller temples dedicated to Vishnu and Shiva. The former has been recently renovated and has a 3-foot-high Deepmala(tower of lamps).The interesting part of the fort is that its wall is completely intact and one can walk along its entire perimeter(about 2 km).Its massive gate is also intact. Several ruins of older structures within the fort still exist. It has six cannons - the largest of which called the Laxmi Toph is located near the Korai Devi temple. The significance of Korigad Fort lies in its historical importance as a strategic stronghold during the Maratha Empire. Built atop a hill in the Western Ghats, it served as a key defense post, guarding the region against potential invaders. Its sturdy fortifications and strategic location allowed it to control trade routes and provide a vantage point for surveillance. Today, it stands as a testament to Maharashtra's rich history and attracts tourists and history enthusiasts who come to explore its ruins and appreciate its architectural marvels.

How to reach?

You can reach the fort via public and private vehicles as it is just around 20 kms from the main town of Lonavala. 
The trek doesn’t start from any base village, and the start point is adjacent to the main road that heads towards Amby Valley.

Via Private Vehicle:

The distance from Mumbai is around 100 kms, and it will take about 2.5-3 hours to reach, considering breaks and traffic jams at the toll nakas.
The distance from Pune is around 85 kms & can take 2-2.5 hours to reach.

Via Public Transport:

You can take a local MSRTC bus to Lonavala from Mumbai or Pune, which will take around 3-4 hours to reach.
Alternatively, you can take a train to Lonavala from Mumbai or Pune. This train journey is an experience in itself, especially during the monsoons.
After reaching Lonavala, you can hire a rickshaw from the main market area and ask them to drop you at the start point of the trek or take you on the road towards Amby Valley.
